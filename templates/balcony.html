You can turn even the tiniest of balconies into an enchanting garden. Use a variety of potted plants and planter boxes on the floor as well as in hanging pots, and utilise every inch of available space to turn your balcony into a green outdoor basis.

Terrace Garden With Pergolas

Anyone who has a rooftop terrace, or even a larger balcony, should definitely go all out to create a charming private garden like this one. The icing on the cake must be the wooden pergolas which filter the light and create interesting patterns of shadows on the floor. What a lovely spot to relax, chat and enjoy your day!

Create A Reading Nook In Your Balcony

A comfy chair and a small side table (or ledge if you don’t have enough space for a table), can turn your balcony into your most favourite spot in the house. You can enjoy the views, curl up with your cuppa, read, write, medi Don’t forget a warm throw to keep you comfortable if you doze off while reading.

Gallery Design With Vertical Garden

Short on space? A vertical garden could be just the right thing to make your area look green. Add shelves or a grill where you can hang small potted plants. You can consider a simple drip watering system to water the plants so that the wall does not get wet. Remember, you could choose to grow some herbs which you can use in your cooking. Try fertilising your plants with kitchen compost; organic is the way to go.

Soak In A Hot Tub

This idea may not be for everyone, especially if your home doesn’t have the kind of privacy required for an outdoor tub. But if you can swing it, the idea of soaking in a hot tub while overlooking a gorgeous view like this is one you shouldn’t pass up.
